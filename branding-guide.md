## Typography

### Display / Brand Name
- **Style:** Bold, wide-spaced serif or display sans
- **Character:** Large, commanding; the letters "Bilal" are tall with generous spacing
- **Use for:** Restaurant name, hero headings

### Subheading / Category Labels
- **Style:** Condensed, all-caps sans-serif
- **Example usage:** "BIRYANI", "OUR SPECIALITY", menu item labels
- **Weight:** Medium to Bold
- **Case:** ALL CAPS

### Body / Address Text
- **Style:** Clean, legible serif or sans-serif
- **Example usage:** Address block, phone number, supporting copy

### Recommended Google Fonts (for web)
- **Display:** Playfair Display (currently in use on the website)
- **Body:** Inter (currently in use on the website)
- **Alternative display:** Cinzel, Cormorant Garamond

## Logo Elements

The logo is composed of three visual layers:

1. **Swoosh Arc** — A bold dark-brown curved stroke evoking a handi (clay pot) silhouette. Color: `#3D1800`.
2. **Brand Name** — "Bilal" in warm gold, large and centered within the arc. Color: `#C4975A`.
3. **Steam Wisps** — Three flowing lines above the logo, in warm gold, suggesting hot, freshly cooked food. Color: `#C4975A`.
4. **Subtitle** — "BIRYANI" in condensed dark-brown caps below the name. Color: `#3D1800`.
5. **Divider Stripes** — Three angled gold/tan bars below the logo, acting as a visual separator. Color: `#BFA06B`.

## Packaging Design Principles

- **Background:** Always use Cream Ivory (`#F5EDD0`) for packaging and print materials to maintain a warm, appetizing feel.
- **Logo Placement:** Centered, upper half of the packaging.
- **Menu Items:** Displayed in small rectangular boxes with dark-brown borders and text on the cream background.
- **Address Banner:** Full-width Burgundy Brown (`#6B2512`) bar at the bottom with Soft Cream (`#FDF6E3`) text.
- **Contact Info:** Top-right corner, dark-brown text on cream background.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use the warm cream-and-gold palette for print materials
- Keep "Bilal" always larger than "BIRYANI" in the logo lockup
- Use all-caps for menu item and category labels
- Pair the logo with the address and phone number on all print assets

### Don't
- Place the logo on a white background — use cream (`#F5EDD0`) instead
- Use the logo in colors other than the defined gold and dark brown
- Mix too many typefaces — stick to display + sans-serif
- Use lowercase for menu item names

## Interior & Ambience (from photos)

The restaurant has two distinct spaces:

1. **Main dining hall** — Geometric LED ceiling lights in white linear patterns; white chair covers with purple ribbon sashes; light walls; spacious and bright.
2. **Booth seating area** — Deep royal-blue feature walls with gold botanical tree and floral patterns; teal green booth sofas; marble-top tables; warm pendant lighting; intimate and atmospheric.

**Key design notes for digital use:**
- The blue-and-gold interior is the most visually distinctive element — this is the "face" of the restaurant.
- The combination of royal blue + warm gold is the true signature colour story, bridging both the packaging (gold) and the interior (blue + gold).
- Use the dark, dramatic palette for the website to mirror the intimate dining room atmosphere.
